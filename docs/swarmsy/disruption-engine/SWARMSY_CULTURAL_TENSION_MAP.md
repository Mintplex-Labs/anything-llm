# SWARMSY Cultural Tension Map

SPARKY must identify what the identity is fighting against before building campaigns.

| Tension | Campaign use | Post hook | Product angle | PR angle | Visual angle | Proof needed | Risk to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Human vs algorithm | Human-made process campaign | “Made by hands, not feed logic.” | Human-crafted editions | Story on human craft in AI era | Imperfect textures, hand marks | Behind-the-scenes process | Anti-tech absolutism or false claims |
| Local vs corporate | Local pride series | “Built here, not boardroom-born.” | Local collab variants | Local economy/community narrative | Neighborhood signage references | Local partner confirmations | Exclusionary or hostile messaging |
| Raw vs polished | Unfiltered build timeline | “Before polish, there is truth.” | Raw edition packaging | Authenticity-over-gloss feature | Grain, rough cuts, draft overlays | Timestamped drafts | Looking low-quality by accident |
| Truth vs fake hype | Proof-first launch | “Proof first. Hype second.” | Proof-tagged offers | Anti-fake narrative | Receipts, logs, real metrics cards | Verifiable proof wall | Shaming named competitors |
| Handmade vs automated sludge | Maker resilience campaign | “Not generated sludge.” | Handmade signatures/marks | Craft survival angle | Tool marks, hand notes | Maker process documentation | Overclaiming “handmade” scope |
| Small creator vs big machine | Independent wave campaign | “Small signal, real force.” | Limited creator drops | Underdog creator narrative | Scale contrast imagery | Real creator story and outputs | Victimhood performance |
| Identity vs invisibility | Visibility declaration campaign | “Seen on purpose.” | Identity-mark bundles | Voice/founder identity interview | Bold face/symbol framing | Consistent publishing artifacts | Performative identity claims |
| Mystery vs oversharing | Clue-led release arc | “You are not meant to know all at once.” | Unlock-based product info | Story on participatory mystery | Hidden glyphs, partial crops | Clue archive and reveals | Deception or fake stakes |
| Community vs audience renting | Ownership loop campaign | “Build with us, not at you.” | Member-influenced editions | Community co-creation narrative | User-contributed boards | Evidence of member impact | Tokenistic participation |
| Underground vs mass market | Niche-first growth strategy | “Not for everyone.” | Collector-first tiers | Cultural niche story | Dark/minimal coded design | Sellout-resistant release logs | Gatekeeping abuse |
| Scarcity vs spam | Fewer, stronger drops | “One drop worth ten posts.” | Limited but truthful runs | Anti-spam product strategy | Numbered assets | Real inventory and drop records | Fake scarcity claims |
| Memory vs feed decay | Archive-centric campaign | “Designed to be remembered.” | Collectible archival products | Preservation and legacy angle | Date stamps, archive grids | Permanent archive links | Nostalgia without substance |
| Signal vs noise | Quality-over-volume campaign | “Signal over noise.” | High-intent product pages | Strategic clarity narrative | Clean dominant symbol systems | Engagement quality metrics | Empty slogans without action |

## Use Rule

Pick 1 primary and 1 secondary tension per campaign. If no clear tension exists, do not launch.

## Integrity Floor

- No fake proof
- No fake engagement/bots/press/sales/community
- No spam or manipulation
- No illegal or reckless guidance
